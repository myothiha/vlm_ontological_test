including anatomical containment, adjacency, interaction, connectivity,
association with systems, pathological involvement, or participation in
diagnostic or clinical workflows.

including a possible instance of the specified class.

- The image may NOT explicitly show all related entities described in the knowledge.
- Do NOT assume that a specific relationship is visible in the image.
- Questions must test whether the VLM understands **what kinds of relationships an entity of this type is known to have**,

**biomedically appropriate ontological supertype or role** whenever possible,
and adapt when the class does not clearly fit a predefined category.

- Anatomical structure → **organ**
- Biological tissue or fluid → **tissue** or **biological fluid**

- Pathological process → **pathological process**

unless no medically meaningful alternative is reasonable.

return an empty JSON list [] and do not generate any questions.

Your task is to generate reasoning-based test questions that:
- Can be answered using **visual identification of an appropriate biomedical entity**

- Refer to the target entity **implicitly**, without naming the class directly
- Follow the **ontology-guided implicit reference policy**, adapting when necessary
- Are explicitly grounded in the provided relational knowledge
- Focus exclusively on **relationships** (e.g., part-of, connected-to, interacts-with, associated-with)
- Do NOT introduce intrinsic properties, functions, or behaviors
- Avoid explanations, answers, or meta-commentary

Return your output as a JSON array containing **only the list of questions**.
